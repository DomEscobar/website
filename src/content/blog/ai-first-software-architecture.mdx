## AI-first is not autopilot

AI-first architecture starts with better questions. A model can propose options, group risks, and reveal missing constraints. The team still owns the decision: which quality attributes matter, which data is sensitive, and which choices are reversible?

## A practical workflow

A reliable AI-first architecture workflow has four steps:

1. Gather context: goals, constraints, existing systems, and non-goals.
2. Generate options: at least two viable approaches with tradeoffs.
3. Review decisions: security, performance, maintainability, cost, and team capability.
4. Document the result: short ADRs, clear terms, sources, and follow-up work.

## Why generative engines can understand it

Generative engines benefit from unambiguous content. Use short answers, explicit definitions, consistent entities, and clear relationships between person, expertise, topic, and project.
